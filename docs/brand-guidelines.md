# Atlas Brand Guidelines

## Brand

Name: Atlas

Tagline: Map your next career move.

Positioning: Atlas helps fresh graduates and early-career professionals turn their resume and a target job description into a clear role-fit report and career roadmap.

## Visual Direction

Atlas should feel professional, calm, and credible. The interface should borrow from clean consulting and data-product UI patterns: quiet typography, restrained colors, structured panels, and clear action hierarchy.

## Colors

- Primary background: `#ffffff`
- Secondary background: `#f8f9fb`
- Tertiary background: `#f0f2f5`
- Primary text: `#0a0c10`
- Secondary text: `#5c6370`
- Muted text: `#8b919e`
- Accent blue: `#0b73b9`
- Accent blue hover: `#0a6ba6`
- Subtle border: `rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.08)`

## Typography

- Primary UI font: Inter
- Optional accent font: Instrument Serif for select display moments
- Use clear, compact headings inside dashboard panels.
- Avoid oversized marketing typography inside the app workspace.

## Components

- Buttons: 6px radius, strong blue primary action, subtle shadow.
- Cards: white or soft gray panels, 8-12px radius, thin border, light shadow.
- Inputs: large text areas with clear labels and validation messages.
- Report sections: scannable panels for score, strengths, gaps, roadmap, and suggestions.

## Voice

- Direct
- Helpful
- Evidence-based
- Practical
- No inflated career promises

Preferred copy style:

- "Map your next career move."
- "Compare your resume to a real target role."
- "Turn gaps into a 30/60/90-day plan."
- "Use this as guidance, not a hiring prediction."
